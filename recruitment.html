  • Why Go Greek

    Despite the negative images of college Greek life, many Greek organizations have quite a lot to offer, both during and after your time in school. If you're thinking of joining a fraternity or sorority, consider the following benefits as you decide. The friendships you build through a fraternity or sorority often have a different "feel" to them than other friendships you make during your time in school. The other members of the chapter really do become your Family. Additionally, Greek Organizations present you with community service opportunities, an academic support network and even a professional network after graduation. In short, Going Greek opens personal and professional doors for you both throughout your collegiate career and the rest of your life afterwards.

  • Recruitment and Rushing

    Northwestern uses a system of deferred recruitment, where the official handing out of bids happens during the first week of Winter Quarter, and all of Fall quarter is used as a period for informal recruitment. If you are interested in joining, get to know some of the guys either at our Sunday Night Dinners or through various clubs and activity groups on campus. Any questions can be directed to our 2014 Recruitment Chair Josh Kriss (joshuakriss2017@u.northwestern.edu). You can also find more information on Northwestern IFC's website
